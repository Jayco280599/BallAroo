Testing

An overview on testing the product

The point of testing all the components, is to make sure that all of these said components work properly and efficiently in order to avoid any risks that may come when someone purchases the product, such as external and internal injuries.

The components worth testing is listed below:

  • Arduino Micro.
  • RGB LED.
  • 2 Digital Encoders.
  • The ultrasound sensor to detect the ball entering the inlet funnel.
  • 3 stepper motors, for the launcher, ball queue, and launch pitch adjusting system.
  • Control solenoid.
  • 1 or 2 high speed DC (Direct Current) motors for the throwing wheel/s.
  • 2 Switches.
  • 2 potentiometers to adjust launch pitch and amplitude.
  • A switch to turn self-training mode on and off.
  • A lithium-ion battery and suitable Battery Management System (BMS).
  • Suitable AC-to-DC power supply for powering and charging the unit.

Giving the final product is completed we create a testing group with people willing to sacrifice arms or legs to the chamber process.

Testing mobile app compatibility and functionality is a crucial factor as we need to make sure that the software is user friendly. We also need to make sure that the app can communicate, with the advanced model of the ball thrower and to make sure that the following features can be displayed without error:
- Battery Status.
- View Status of the dog treat dispenser.
- Recording of usage stats (Amount pet has used the device).
- The ability of object recognition (it must know that it is picking up a ball and not something else).
- The Ability to check for any obstructions within the device.
