Targets

Aim, Scope and Limits

Aim:

The BallAroo is intended to an automated smart device of which a dog, or person, can put a ball into a feeder at the top of unit and the ball is launched to a set distance. The system could then dispense a treat as an incentive or training aid to encourage the dog to keep using the unit. This device can be used in a large yard, a small yard or taken to the local park or beach. It is intended to be the perfect companion for the owner and the dog, wherever they may be. This device will be compact with a rechargeable power supply for easy portability, and it will be easily adjusted from just 2 dials.

The main goal of the initial BallAroo is to produce the first in a line of a family of products to gain a foothold into the pet care industry. The initial product model would target the sub $100 AUD market, to keep the unit affordable to ensure mass market appeal. This would mean the unit would be kept frugal in the feature list, but with right engineering approach, the unit can serve as a base for future up-market feature-packed models.

Scope and Limits:

Abilities of the project

The project is capable in building a ball thrower. The engineering involved is understood and the componentry are considered readily available. The large variety of available small board computers and integrated circuits on the market can enable addition of features while remaining cost effective.

Limitations of the project

The project limitations are not engineering or technical, but economical. The selection of included components and features are going to be based on targeted sale price rather than technical reasons. The initial version of the BallAroo is intended to be a budget ball thrower aimed at the sub $100 AUD range, which means included features are going to be selected to keep to the targeted price bracket. More advanced features and their associated higher costs may be reserved for future versions of the BallAroo.
