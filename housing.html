Housing

The final version of BallAroo's outer housing would be designed to use a cheap, lightweight but durable plastic to protect the mechanics and electronics housed inside, while remaining pleasing to the eye. Though the initial prototyping phase, a simple skeleton type frame would be utilised to mount the various components of design in their intended positions. The skeleton frame, though unwieldly in appearance, would facilitate ease of access and rapid changes in design geometry. Once the basic functionality has been tested and the design geometry has been established, the design of the housing and components that are to be integrated into the housing can begin. The use of CAD (Computer Aided Design) and 3D printing technology would be instrumental to accelerate the final design of the unit. The design can be tweaked during product testing to ensure the unit aesthetically pleasing while remaining functional and durable.
